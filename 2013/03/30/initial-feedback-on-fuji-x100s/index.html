Initial feedback on Fuji X100S

2013-03-30
Photography
Hardware · Review

My X100S has finally arrived. First time I get a brand new camera that I’m already familiar with. Many reviews have now been written on this fantastic camera. I just wanted to share some of my initial thoughts based upon my first experiments done at night and under difficult light.

What I liked

  • Overall speed is much improved.
  • Reduced minimum focus speed without going macro is just great.
  • High ISO pictures are very clean.
  • Autofocus seems much faster than before but under low light and when using the smallest focus area there are some misses.
  • Much better menu layout. Could be further improved though.
  • Being able to shoot 1:1 picture is great so I can practice my composition like I do on my 6x6 film camera.
  • A paper version of the user manual was in the box this time.
  • No longer needed to tape the little piece of plastic that holds the battery when placed on the charger. It stays on its own now.
  • Focus peaking is an interesting feature that I might be using more than expected.
  • Pressing the shutter all the way down without pre-focusing works really well (P&S style).

What I disliked

  • Not possible to shoot 1:1 in RAW mode [EDIT] I does seem to work and the best part of it is that you get the 3:2 RAW version of the photo next to the 1:1 JPEG version.
  • Box that contains the X100S looks much cheaper than the previous classy hard case.
  • Having three banks of custom settings is great but not all the parameters are saved such as picture resolution and flash mode.
  • Impossible to convert a RAW picture to JPEG using one of the new so called creative filters (toy camera, high-key…). Filter needs to be selected upon taking the picture. Not a big deal but still don’t get the limitation!
  • RAW files are much bigger than on X100 (33MB vs 19MB). Wondering why they haven’t used some kind of lossless compression for .RAF files.
  • Still no menu option to easily set the lens to hyperfocal. This should be fairly trivial on a fixed and non-removable lens.
  • Still no face detection for focus selection, therefore the AF multi option is not very usable.
  • When using AF-S and shooting video, it is not possible to change adjust the focus when recording has started. You need to switch to AF-C prior to shoot video if you want continuous focus.
  • I don’t understand the new total exposure counter considering that it takes into consideration not only shot pictures but also power-off/power-on sequences and OVF/EVF switchings.
  • ISO bracketing, film simulation bracketing and DR bracketing are not available when shooting F+RAW (only with Fine only). This would have been great for testing those new film simulation options (ProNeg H and ProNeg Std).
  • When formating SD cards with the X100S, FAT32 filesystem is used on 16GB cards (not tested on 32GB) while exFAT is used on 64GB cards which brings a number of limitations. Check out my other post regarding this specific exFAT story and how to format your 64GB card with FAT32.

PS: You might have noticed that the opening picture is my old X100 (look at the shape of the OVF/EVF lever). Since they share the same optic I thought it would still be ok to use it there.
